Pellets


Made from compressed organic materials like wood or agricultural residues, bio-pellets serve as a renewable and eco-friendly fuel source. They offer high energy efficiency and produce low emissions, making them ideal for heating systems and industrial processes focused on reducing their carbon footprint.

CNG


This alternative to gasoline and diesel is used mainly in vehicles, delivering cleaner combustion and lower greenhouse gas emissions. CNG is widely adopted for both public and private transport, offering a more sustainable option with reduced impact on air quality.

Ethanol


Derived from crops such as corn and sugarcane, ethanol is a renewable biofuel frequently blended with gasoline. It’s commonly used to lower emissions in cars, offering a cleaner alternative by reducing pollutants and dependence on fossil fuels while supporting agricultural economies.

BioFuel Fertilizer

Created as a byproduct of biofuel production, this nutrient-rich fertilizer supports sustainable agriculture by enhancing soil quality and crop yields. Its use not only recycles organic material but also minimizes reliance on synthetic fertilizers, promoting eco-friendly farming practices.
